Running games are a subgenre of action or arcade games that involve controlling a character who is constantly moving forward, with the player’s main objective being to navigate obstacles, avoid hazards, and collect items. These games, like Temple Run and Subway Surfers, typically feature simple controls—often just swiping or tapping—to jump, slide, or change direction as the character progresses through increasingly difficult levels or environments. The goal is often to survive as long as possible while achieving high scores or completing objectives. Running games emphasize fast reflexes, quick decision-making, and timing, offering an addictive, fast-paced experience that appeals to both casual and competitive players.
